AI Visibility Checklists by Business Type
Practical checklists for making a business easier for AI systems to identify, understand, and describe using available public evidence.
Created by Daizie.ai, a Practical Informatics product built and calibrated by Marty Koepke.
What these checklists cover
Correct business and entity identification
Visibility in relevant buyer questions
Accuracy and currency of AI answers
Recommendation fit
Category-specific sources and evidence
Important limitations and exclusions
Consistency across public sources
Important limitation
These are discovery recipes, not universal platform requirements. Different AI systems may use different sources, retrieval methods, and ranking processes.
Completing a checklist does not guarantee that an AI system will mention, cite, rank, or recommend a business. Recommendations should follow the business’s actual measured gaps.
Shared foundation
These checks apply across local businesses, service businesses, and products.
1. Establish a clear identity

Use the same business or product name consistently.

Identify the official website and canonical domain.

State what the business or product provides in plain English.

Distinguish the business from similarly named entities.

Provide an accurate About page.

Connect relevant people, companies, products, and locations.

Correct outdated names, addresses, offers, and profiles.
2. Explain the offering

Identify the intended audience.

Explain the primary services, products, or use cases.

Maintain a dedicated page for each important offering.

State meaningful requirements and limitations.

Explain who is—and is not—a good fit.

Avoid unsupported superlatives such as “best” or “leading.”
3. Keep important information current

Publish current contact and availability information.

Publish pricing or explain how pricing is determined.

Date information that can become outdated.

Correct inaccurate third-party profiles where possible.

Remove outdated or unverifiable claims.

Keep important information consistent across authoritative sources.
4. Provide supporting evidence

Link important claims to relevant evidence.

Separate company claims from independent evidence.

Include dates and source names where appropriate.

Preserve the context and limitations of statistics.

Use legitimate reviews and testimonials.

Never purchase, fabricate, or manipulate reviews or endorsements.
5. Support technical discovery

Provide a current sitemap.

Avoid accidentally blocking important public pages.

Set canonical URLs.

Use descriptive page titles and headings.

Make important information available as readable HTML.

Use relevant structured data accurately.

Ensure structured data agrees with visible page content.
Local-business checklist
For bakeries, plumbers, dentists, restaurants, contractors, and other location- or service-area-based businesses.
Depending on the AI system and buyer question, potentially relevant sources may include Google Business Profile and map data, local directories, customer reviews, chambers, trade associations, local coverage, and the business’s website.
1. Confirm identity and location

Use a consistent official business name.

Keep the address and phone number current.

Use consistent name, address, and phone information across profiles.

Identify every active location on the website.

State service areas explicitly.

Connect map listings to the canonical website.

Correct former addresses, phone numbers, and locations.

Distinguish the business from similarly named local businesses.
2. Explain services and availability

Clearly list the services or products provided.

Give each important service enough detail to understand it.

Publish current business hours.

Explain appointment, delivery, or emergency availability.

Provide pricing or useful pricing cues where appropriate.

State important exclusions and limitations.

Keep seasonal or temporary information current.

Maintain useful location or service-area pages.
3. Maintain local profiles

Complete and verify the Google Business Profile.

Keep map categories and business information current.

Maintain accurate profiles in relevant local directories.

Maintain chamber and trade-association listings.

Correct duplicate or inaccurate listings where possible.

Use consistent descriptions across authoritative profiles.

Link each profile to the correct website and location.
4. Establish local evidence

Invite real customers to leave honest reviews.

Maintain recent reviews that describe actual experiences.

Respond appropriately to legitimate customer feedback.

Preserve a natural distribution of reviews over time.

Link relevant local coverage and community mentions.

Document appropriate licenses, certifications, or memberships.

Never manufacture reviews, local mentions, or endorsements.
5. Support local technical discovery

Use the appropriate LocalBusiness schema type.

Ensure schema accurately reflects the business.

Match structured addresses and hours to visible page content.

Use unique and useful content on location pages.

Keep location information available as readable HTML.

Include relevant locations in the sitemap.
Key diagnostic question
Can AI confidently identify this business, where it operates, what it provides, and whether available evidence indicates that local customers trust it?
Service-business checklist
For consultants, coaches, agencies, professional services, personal brands, and other expertise-based businesses.
Depending on the AI system and buyer question, potentially relevant sources may include the business website, professional profiles, association directories, case studies, articles, interviews, podcasts, videos, and independent mentions.
1. Connect the person and business

Connect the expert, company, and current offer clearly.

Explain the founder’s or practitioner’s current role.

Use consistent identity and offer language across profiles.

Distinguish the expert from similarly named people.

Maintain an accurate professional biography.

Connect former company names or roles where relevant.

Make the canonical professional profile clear.
2. Define expertise and audience

Describe expertise specifically.

Identify the intended audience.

Explain the problems the service addresses.

Maintain detailed pages for important services.

Explain the engagement model or methodology.

State who is—and is not—a good fit.

Explain geography, delivery format, and availability.

Publish pricing or engagement expectations where appropriate.
3. Support credibility

Keep credentials current and verifiable.

Document relevant education, licenses, or certifications.

Maintain accurate association-directory profiles.

Publish useful articles demonstrating relevant expertise.

Connect interviews, podcasts, and videos to the relevant subject.

Link independent mentions to the correct person and company.

Avoid vague or unsupported authority claims.
4. Document experience and outcomes

Publish case studies with appropriate customer permission.

Explain the context and work performed in each case study.

Support outcome claims with available evidence.

Preserve relevant limitations and context.

Use genuine and attributable testimonials.

Separate customer statements from company claims.

Avoid implying that one client outcome is universally reproducible.
5. Maintain professional consistency

Keep LinkedIn and other authoritative profiles current.

Use consistent descriptions of expertise and services.

Remove obsolete offers and outdated biographies.

Connect current services to relevant public work.

Make contact and inquiry information easy to find.

Correct inaccurate third-party descriptions where possible.
Key diagnostic question
Can AI explain who this expert is, whom they help, why available evidence supports their credibility, and when they should be recommended?
Product and SaaS checklist
For software platforms, apps, physical products, marketplaces, and other product-based businesses.
Depending on the AI system and buyer question, potentially relevant sources may include product pages, documentation, help centers, review platforms, software databases, product directories, independent reviews, communities, integration directories, GitHub, app stores, and marketplaces.
1. Explain the product

Explain the product in one plain-English sentence.

Identify the intended users.

Describe the primary use cases.

Maintain clear feature and capability pages.

Distinguish capabilities from planned features.

Publish current pricing and plan differences.

Explain free trials, free plans, or usage limits.

State important technical or operational requirements.

Disclose material limitations.

Distinguish the product from similarly named products.
2. Support product evaluation

Maintain useful documentation.

Publish a help center or support information.

Answer real evaluation questions in an FAQ.

Explain implementation and onboarding requirements.

Document integrations accurately.

Maintain current privacy and security information.

Explain cancellation, support, and data-export policies.

Keep public documentation consistent with the current product.
3. Explain differentiation and fit

State how the product differs using factual criteria.

Explain which audience or use case it serves best.

Explain when the product is not the right choice.

Discuss alternatives fairly.

Keep comparison pages current.

Support comparison claims with appropriate evidence.

Avoid unsupported “best,” “leading,” or superiority claims.
4. Maintain product profiles

Maintain accurate G2, Capterra, or relevant marketplace profiles.

Use current descriptions in product directories.

Keep Product Hunt information current where relevant.

Maintain GitHub profiles and repositories where relevant.

Maintain accurate app-store or marketplace listings.

Maintain integration and partner-directory profiles.

Correct obsolete pricing, screenshots, and descriptions.
5. Establish independent evidence

Invite legitimate customers to leave honest reviews.

Separate independent reviews from company claims.

Respond appropriately to customer feedback.

Participate constructively in relevant communities.

Support real user discussion without directing the conclusion.

Link relevant independent reviews and analyst coverage.

Never manufacture reviews, ratings, stars, votes, or discussions.

Disclose paid placements, sponsorships, and affiliate relationships.
6. Support product discovery

Use accurate SoftwareApplication, Product, or other relevant schema.

Ensure structured data agrees with visible product information.

Make important product information available as readable HTML.

Include product, feature, use-case, pricing, and documentation pages in the sitemap.

Set canonical URLs for product and documentation pages.

Avoid hiding essential evaluation information behind scripts or sign-in walls where practical.
Key diagnostic question
Can AI understand what the product does, who it is for, how it differs, and whether credible users or independent sources support that understanding?
How Daizie assesses AI visibility
Across all three categories, Daizie assesses four layers:
Identity: Did AI find the correct business?
Visibility: Does the business appear in relevant buyer questions?
Accuracy: Is what AI says correct, current, and supported by the available evidence?
Recommendation fit: Does AI understand when—and when not—to recommend the business?
These checklists provide starting points. A Daizie assessment measures the business’s actual results and identifies the gaps supported by the available evidence.
